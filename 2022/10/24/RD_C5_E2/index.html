Rudin Chap.5 Differentiation

Exercise 6

Suppose

  • \(f\) is continuous for \(x\ge 0\),
  • \(f'(x)\) exists for \(x>0\),
  • \(f(0)=0\),
  • \(f'\) is monotonically increasing.

Put \[ g(x)=\dfrac{f(x)}{x}\quad(x>0) \] and prove that \(g\) is monotonically increasing.

Solution

To prove that \(g\) is monotonically increasing, we just need to show that \[ g'(x)=\dfrac{xf'(x)-f(x)}{x^2}\geq0 \] for \(x>0\).

By the mean value theorem, for \(x>0\), there exists \(\xi\in (0,x)\) such that \[ f(x)-f(0)=f(x)=xf'(\xi) \] therefore \[ xf'(x)-f(x)=xf'(x)-xf'(\xi)\geq0 \] since \(f'\) is monotonically increasing.

Exercise 7

Suppose \(f'(x),g'(x)\) exist, \(g'(x)\neq 0\), and \(f(x)=g(x)=0\). Prove that \[ \lim_{t\to x}\dfrac{f(t)}{g(t)}=\dfrac{f'(x)}{g'(x)} \]

Solution

Since \(f'(x),g'(x)\) exist and \(f(x)=g(x)=0\), we have \[ \lim_{t\to x}\dfrac{f(t)}{g(t)}=\lim_{t\to x}\dfrac{\frac{f(t)-f(x)}{t-x}}{\frac{g(t)-g(x)}{t-x}}=\dfrac{f'(x)}{g'(x)} \]

Exercise 8

Suppose \(f'\) is continuous on \([a,b]\) and \(\varepsilon >0\). Prove that there exists \(\delta >0\) such that \[ \left|\dfrac{f(t)-f(x)}{t-x}-f'(x)\right|<\varepsilon \] whenever \(0<|t-x|<\delta,a\le x\le b,a\le t\le b\). Does this hold for vector-valued functions?

Solution

since \(f'\) is continuous on \([a,b]\) and \([a,b]\) is compact, \(f'\) is uniformly continuous on \([a,b]\).

By definition of uniformly continuous there exists \(\delta >0\) such that \[ |f(t)-f(x)|<\varepsilon \] for every \(x,t\in [a,b]\) and \(|t-x|<\delta\). Suppose \(t<x\), by the mean value theorem there exists \(\xi\in(t,x)\) such that \[ f(x)-f(t)=(x-t)f'(\xi) \] Thus \[ \left|\dfrac{f(x)-f(t)}{x-t}-f'(x)\right|=|f'(\xi)-f'(x)|<\varepsilon \] In the case \(t>x\) the argument is similar.

The conclusion for vector-valued function also holds. Suppose \(\mathbf{f}(x)=(f_1(x),f_2(x),\cdots,f_k(x))\). for \(i=1,2,\dots,k\) there exists \(\delta_i>0\) such that \[ \left|\dfrac{f_i(x)-f_i(t)}{x-t}-f_i'(x)\right|<\dfrac{\varepsilon}{\sqrt k} \] if \(0<|t-x|<\delta_i\).

Thus we have \[ \left|\dfrac{\mathbf{f}(x)-\mathbf{f}(t)}{x-t}-\mathbf{f}'(x)\right|=\left|\left(\dfrac{f_1(x)-f_1(t)}{x-t}-f_1'(x),\dfrac{f_2(x)-f_2(t)}{x-t}-f_2'(x),\cdots,\dfrac{f_k(x)-f_k(t)}{x-t}-f_k'(x)\right)\right|\leq \sqrt{\sum_{j=1}^k\left|\dfrac{f_j(x)-f_j(t)}{x-t}-f_j'(x)\right|^2}<\sqrt{\sum_{j=1}^k\dfrac{\varepsilon^2}{k}}=\varepsilon \]
